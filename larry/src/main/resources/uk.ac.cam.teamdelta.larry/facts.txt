There were almost 36 million licensed older drivers in 2012, which is a 34 percent increase from 1999
Per mile traveled, fatal crash rates increase noticeably starting at ages 70 to 74 and are highest among drivers age 85
and older
Older drivers tend to limit their driving during bad weather and at night and drive fewer miles than younger drivers
Since older drivers are more fragile, their fatality rates are 17 times higher than those aged 25 to 64
